Metaphorical Analysis of Etiology，Pathogenesis，and Treatment of“Hives”
DAI Ming1，JIA Chunhua2
(1． School of Chinese medicine，Xinjiang Medical University，Urumqi830000; 2． School of Chinese medicine，Beijing University of
Chinese Medicine)
ABSTＲACT From the perspective of metaphor，this paper observed the clinical manifestations of“hives”，expoun-
ded its etiology，pathogenesis，principles and treatment，and explored the metaphorical features of them to find the
possible cognitive roots of various treatments． Starting from the name and clinical manifestations of“hives”，the wind
in different seasons of nature and the phenomena caused by wind are taken as the original domain，while the clinical
manifestations of hives are taken as the target domain，and the“pathogenic wind”experienced by human is recog-
nized through the natural wind of the original domain． Wind from the nature has obvious seasonality，and people can
obviously perceive the differences of the wind in different seasons． The wind and the natural phenomena associated
with wind produced by the nature are likely to be the cognitive root of“wind-cold，wind-dampness，wind-heat，and
wind-dryness”in traditional Chinese medicine． The protection against wind and accompanying phenomena is likely to
be the starting point for the treatment of hives．
Keywords hives; metaphor; pathogenic wind; phenomenon of wind; etiology and pathogenesis of disease; treatment
